why emberctl

The discipline of a CLI, the comfort of a kitchen.

Rigid monospace structure, but output that reads like a friend telling you it went fine.

01

One warm config

A single hearth.toml describes every task. Readable, commented, and forgiving of typos.

02

Gentle output

Amber checkmarks, soft timing, and no stack-trace screaming. Errors arrive with a note, not a wall.

03

Kindling cache

Tasks remember their last run and skip what is already done. Cold starts become warm restarts.

the workflow

Four commands. A whole tended day.

~/projects/cabin — a typical session
$ emberctl init

Lay the fire

Scaffolds a hearth.toml from your repo, detecting language, scripts, and the tasks you already run by hand.

$ emberctl run

Light it

Runs any named task or chain. Output streams in warm amber with live timing and a tidy summary box.

$ emberctl watch

Keep it lit

Re-runs tasks on file changes, debounced and quiet, so the room stays calm while you keep typing.

$ emberctl bank

Bank the coals

Caches results and prints a cozy end-of-day digest of everything that ran, saved, and stayed warm.
